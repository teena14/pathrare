PathRare User Flows

This document details the user journeys and flows currently built into the platform.

1. Authentication Flow
- Step 1 (Role Selection): When launching the application, the user selects 1 of 3 roles (Patient, NGO, Volunteer). 
- Step 2 (Auth Action): The user toggles between Login or Sign-up mode. They authenticate using either Email/Password or a Google account. The flow strictly detects role conflicts to prevent mismatches.
- Step 3 (Profile Completion): Post-authentication, the user fills out a role-specific "complete-profile" form.
- Step 4 (Redirection): The application checks the Firestore `role` parameter and seamlessly redirects the user to their role-specific dashboard (e.g., `/patient`, `/ngo`).

2. Patient User Flows
- Primary Navigation: Patients land on `/patient`, which provides routing to primary utilities via tabs: Life Assist, Diagnostic, Clinical Profile, Community, and Care. It contains a reliable "Back to Dashboard" loop.
- Diagnostic Flow (/patient/diagnose):
  - The patient decides to either upload clinical documents or provide text-based symptoms directly.
  - The backend pipeline executes OCR (via Google Vision API) if a document is uploaded, then extracts symptoms using an NLP engine (Gemini).
  - The extracted data is matched via Vertex AI text embeddings against established Orphanet datasets.
  - The patient receives ranked cards with matching diagnoses and inference data.
- Life Assist Flow (/patient/life-assist):
  - The patient chooses one of 4 categories: Financial & Legal, Adaptive Education, Medical & Care, or Assistive Technology.
  - The patient sees a list of suggested actions ranked by a multi-factor priority score ((Urgency × 0.4) + (Risk × 0.3) + (Delay × 0.2) + (Access × 0.1)).
  - Behind the scenes, the request goes through a 4-tier allocation engine (Tier 1: Volunteer → Tier 2: NGO → Tier 3: Self-guided → Tier 4: Queue) ensuring the user is never left at zero dead-ends and receives the best actionable path.

3. Volunteer User Flow
- Volunteer Dashboard (/volunteer): After authentication, the volunteer lands on their dashboard equipped with a match-scored prioritized task feed.
- Action Flow: They can choose to accept specific tasks (pushed down from patient Life Assist requests), interact with the task parameters, and review their own reliability and impact statistics.

4. NGO User Flow
- NGO Dashboard (/ngo): Organization representatives land on their dashboard showing task batches that require institutional help.
- Analytics Flow: NGOs can assess regional demand dynamics and overall organizational success statistics.
